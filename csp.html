Child Safety Policy for nostrlabs.io

Commitment to Child Safety

nostrlabs.io is dedicated to ensuring a safe and secure environment for all users of nostrlabs.io, with a particular focus on protecting children from child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and child sexual abuse material (CSAM). We strictly prohibit any content or behavior that sexually exploits, abuses, or endangers children, including but not limited to grooming, sextortion, trafficking, or sharing CSAM. Our policies align with global norms, including the Tech Coalition’s best practices for combating online CSEA, and comply with all applicable laws, such as the U.S. Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Definitions

  • CSAE (Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation): Any content or behavior that sexually exploits, abuses, or endangers children, including grooming, sextortion, or trafficking for sexual purposes.
  • CSAM (Child Sexual Abuse Material): Illegal material depicting child sexual abuse, prohibited under our Terms of Service and Google Play policies.

Published Standards Against CSAE

We maintain a zero-tolerance policy for CSAE and CSAM. Our standards include:

  • Prohibited Content: Users are strictly prohibited from creating, uploading, or sharing content that exploits or endangers children, including CSAM or material promoting predatory behavior.
  • User Conduct: Any behavior targeting children inappropriately, such as grooming or soliciting explicit content, is banned.
  • Enforcement: Upon detection or report of CSAM or CSAE, we take immediate action, including content removal, account termination, and reporting to authorities like the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) as required by law.
  • Monitoring and Moderation: We employ a combination of automated systems and human moderation to detect and prevent CSAE and CSAM, ensuring compliance with our policies and legal obligations.

In.App Feedback Mechanism

To facilitate user reports of CSAE, CSAM, or other safety concerns, nostrlabs.io provides an in-app feedback mechanism accessible without leaving the app. Users can:

  • Navigate to the “Report a Concern” section in the app’s settings or profile menu.
  • Submit reports via an in-app form, support email (policy@nostrlabs.io), or live chat channel.
  • Receive confirmation of their report and updates on actions taken, where applicable.

This mechanism is certified in the Google Play Console as part of our compliance with the Child Safety Standards policy.

Taking Appropriate Action

When we obtain actual knowledge of CSAM or CSAE within nostrlabs.io, we act swiftly in accordance with our published standards and relevant laws. Actions include:

  • Immediate removal of offending content.
  • Suspension or termination of accounts involved in CSAE or CSAM.
  • Reporting to law enforcement and child safety organizations, such as NCMEC, as required.
  • Cooperation with Google Play and authorities to ensure compliance and user safety.

Compliance with Google Play Policies

nostrlabs.io is fully compliant with Google Play’s Child Safety Standards policy, effective as of April 2024, and the Child Endangerment policy preview (effective January 22, 2025). We self-certify our adherence to these standards in the Google Play Console. Additionally, our app complies with the Families Policy if targeting children, ensuring age-appropriate content and adherence to COPPA, GDPR, and other relevant regulations.

User Data and Privacy

For apps targeting children or collecting personal data, we provide a comprehensive Privacy Policy linked in the app and on our Google Play Store listing. This policy details:

  • Data collection practices (e.g., personal information, location, or media).
  • Security measures, such as encryption in transit.
  • Data deletion options, allowing users to request account and data removal.
  • Compliance with the Data Safety Form requirements, ensuring transparency about data handling.

Reporting Violations

If you believe a child is in danger or has been subject to abuse, exploitation, or trafficking, please contact local law enforcement immediately and report to a child safety organization, such as those listed at Google’s Child Safety Resources. To report CSAM or CSAE within nostrlabs.io, use the in-app reporting mechanism or contact our CSAM point of contact directly.
